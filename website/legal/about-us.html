About UsDocuScan OCR

Our Mission

Most document scanner apps either upload your documents to the cloud for processing (a privacy risk), lock essential features behind subscriptions, or store your data in proprietary formats that are hard to escape. DocuScan OCR was created to offer a different path.

DocuScan OCR is an offline-first, privacy-respecting document scanning application for Android. We believe that:

  • Your documents belong to you. They should not be uploaded to a third-party server for processing unless you explicitly choose to sync them to a cloud you control.
  • OCR should be free. Text recognition is a basic utility, not a premium feature. ML Kit makes on-device OCR accessible to any Android app, and we built on that foundation.
  • Encryption should be the default, not the upsell. Private folders with AES-256-GCM encryption are built-in, not a paid upgrade.
  • Your data should be portable. Every document can be exported to PDF, DOCX, or TXT — widely supported open formats that you can open with any tool, not just this app.
  • Cloud sync should go to your accounts. We don't operate a backend server and we don't want your subscription money. If you want backups, configure your own Google Drive, Dropbox, or Cloudflare R2.

What Makes DocuScan OCR Different

  • 7 runtime dependencies. No Firebase, no Retrofit, no Moshi, no Coil, no Hilt — just the minimum needed to do the job. A smaller dependency footprint means a smaller attack surface and faster builds.
  • Real on-device OCR. The same ML Kit model that powers Google's own apps runs on your device. No network requests, no API costs, no data leaving your phone.
  • Real encryption. Android Keystore master key + PBKDF2 + AES-256-GCM. Verified by source code inspection, not by marketing claims.
  • Real cloud sync. Connect your own Google Drive, Dropbox, or R2 bucket. The app doesn't proxy through any server — uploads go directly from your device to the provider.
  • Zero data collection. No analytics, no crash reporting, no telemetry, no advertising ID. There is no backend server to receive your data.
  • Free to use. No paid tiers, no in-app purchases, no ads. The full feature set — OCR, encryption, export, cloud sync — is available to all users at no cost.

Technical Overview

DocuScan OCR is written in 100% Kotlin using Jetpack Compose with Material Design 3. It follows the MVVM architecture pattern with a single ViewModel serving all screens. The app uses Room (SQLite) for persistent storage, CameraX for live camera capture, ML Kit for on-device OCR, OkHttp for cloud API calls, and the Android Keystore for encryption key management.

The app targets Android 7.0 (API 24) as its minimum supported version and Android 16 (API 36) as its target. The codebase is built with Gradle 9.1.0 and Android Gradle Plugin 9.1.1.
