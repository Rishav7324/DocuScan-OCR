2. OCR Accuracy Disclaimer

The optical character recognition (OCR) functionality in DocuScan OCR is powered by Google ML Kit's on-device text recognition model. While ML Kit is a sophisticated machine learning system, OCR is inherently imperfect. The accuracy of text recognition depends on numerous factors, including:

  • Image quality, resolution, and lighting conditions during capture.
  • Document type, font, handwriting quality, and language.
  • Presence of noise, shadows, glare, or obstructions in the image.
  • Perspective distortion and warping before correction.
  • The specific version of the ML Kit model downloaded on your device.

You should always verify OCR output manually before relying on it for any purpose, including but not limited to:

  • Legal documents, contracts, or agreements.
  • Medical records, prescriptions, or clinical notes.
  • Financial records, invoices, or tax documents.
  • Identification documents, passports, or licenses.
  • Any document where transcription errors could result in legal, financial, or personal harm.

The developer is not liable for any errors, omissions, or misinterpretations resulting from the OCR functionality.

3. Encryption & Security Disclaimer

The App implements encryption features using Android Keystore, AES-256-GCM, and PBKDF2 key derivation. These are industry-standard cryptographic primitives. However:

  • No encryption is absolute. Advances in cryptography, vulnerabilities in the Android platform, or physical attacks on your device may compromise encrypted data.
  • Encryption does not protect against all threats. Keylogging, screen recording, shoulder surfing, or malware on your device can bypass encryption at the point of data capture.
  • The developer is not responsible for data loss due to forgotten PINs. If you forget the PIN for a private folder, the encrypted data cannot be recovered. There is no backdoor, password reset, or recovery mechanism.
  • The developer is not responsible for data breaches that result from vulnerabilities in the App, the Android operating system, or your cloud storage providers.

For a detailed analysis of the security measures and their limitations, see the Security & Responsible Disclosure Policy and the Privacy Policy §15 (Security Measures).
