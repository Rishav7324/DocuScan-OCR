1. Purpose

This Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) defines the rules and restrictions governing your use of DocuScan OCR. Because the App is a local, offline-first tool, the developer does not have the ability to monitor, review, or remove content stored on your device. This policy therefore states the developer's expectations and terms of use, with the understanding that enforcement against local violations is necessarily limited.

2. Prohibited Content

You must not use the App to scan, capture, store, or distribute any content that falls into the following categories:

2.1 Child Exploitation

Zero tolerance. You must not use the App to create, store, or distribute any sexually suggestive or exploitative content involving minors (any individual under 18 years of age). This includes child sexual abuse material (CSAM), child erotica, or any content that sexually exploits or endangers children. Violations will be reported to the appropriate authorities, including but not limited to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) and local law enforcement.

2.2 Illegal Content

You must not use the App to store or distribute:

  • Content that violates any applicable criminal law, including laws against terrorism, hate speech, and incitement to violence.
  • Fraudulent documents, including forged identification, counterfeit currency, fake academic credentials, or any material created with the intent to deceive or defraud.
  • Stolen or unlawfully obtained personal information, including credit card numbers, social security numbers, passports, and driver's licenses belonging to third parties without their consent.
  • Content that depicts non-consensual intimate imagery ("revenge porn") or any other violation of privacy.
  • Malware, viruses, or any code designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access to computer systems.

2.3 Copyright-Infringing Content

You must not use the App to reproduce, store, or distribute copyrighted material without the permission of the copyright owner, except as permitted by fair use, fair dealing, or similar exceptions under applicable copyright law. This includes:

  • Scanned copies of entire books, textbooks, or other copyrighted publications for which you do not hold the rights.
  • Reproducing and distributing copyrighted images, photographs, or artwork without the creator's permission.
  • Systematically copying and redistributing copyrighted software documentation or code.

It is your responsibility to understand the copyright laws applicable in your jurisdiction and to ensure that your scanning and storage of documents does not infringe others' rights.

2.4 Harmful or Dangerous Content

You must not use the App to store or distribute content that promotes or provides instruction for:

  • Violence, terrorism, or organized crime.
  • Self-harm or suicide.
  • Manufacturing weapons, explosives, or illegal drugs.
  • Human trafficking or modern slavery.
  • Genocide or crimes against humanity.

3. Prohibited Conduct

In addition to the content restrictions above, you must not engage in the following activities in connection with the App:

3.1 Security Violations

  • Attempting to bypass, disable, or defeat the App's encryption or security features, except for legitimate security research conducted under the terms of the Responsible Disclosure Policy.
  • Distributing modified versions of the App that remove, disable, or weaken the encryption features without clearly documenting such changes.
  • Reverse-engineering the App to extract API keys, OAuth client secrets embedded in the code, or other credentials (if you discover any embedded credentials through legitimate means, disclose them via the Responsible Disclosure Policy).

3.2 Abuse of Third-Party Services

  • Using the App to violate the terms of service of any integrated third-party service, including Google Drive, Dropbox, and Cloudflare R2.
  • Using the App's cloud sync feature to upload content that the provider's terms prohibit (e.g., uploading copyright-infringing material to Google Drive or Dropbox).

3.3 Misrepresentation

  • Misrepresenting the capabilities of the App (e.g., claiming it is HIPAA-compliant, FDA-cleared, or certified for any regulatory purpose, unless you have obtained such certification independently).
  • Distributing modified versions of the App under the name "DocuScan OCR" in a way that implies official endorsement from the developer.

4. Enforcement & Reporting

Because the App operates locally and the developer does not host a server, the developer's ability to enforce this AUP against content stored on your device is limited. The developer cannot:

  • Monitor or review content stored in the App.
  • Remotely access or delete content from your device.
  • Prevent you from using the App in violation of this AUP.

The developer relies on your compliance with this AUP as a condition of using the App. If the developer becomes aware of a violation (through a report from law enforcement, a third party, or other reliable means), the developer may:

  • Issue a warning requesting that you cease the violating activity.
  • Publicly identify the violating use case (without identifying you personally) to warn other users, if deemed appropriate.
  • Cease distribution of the App to you specifically (e.g., by blocking your access where technically feasible..
  • Report the violation to appropriate law enforcement authorities if the content involves child exploitation, terrorism, or other serious criminal activity.

Serious or repeated violations may result in permanent revocation of your license to use the App.

5. How to Report Violations

If you become aware of content created or distributed using DocuScan OCR that violates this AUP, please report it to:

  • Email: support@prontly.in

Please include in your report:

  • A description of the violating content and why it violates this AUP.
  • Any evidence you have (do not include the violating content itself unless necessary).
  • The date and time you encountered the content.
  • Your contact information (optional, but helpful for follow-up).

The developer will review all reports and take appropriate action. Reports of child exploitation material will be forwarded to law enforcement. Reports may also be forwarded to the relevant cloud provider if the content is stored or shared through the App's cloud sync feature.

6. Contact

If you have questions about this Acceptable Use Policy, please contact us at support@prontly.in.
