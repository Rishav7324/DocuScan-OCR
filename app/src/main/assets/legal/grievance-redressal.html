Grievance Redressal Policy

Last updated: July 18, 2026

Notice for Indian Users: This Grievance Redressal Policy is published in compliance with the Information Technology Act, 2000 and the Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021. Users in India may use this mechanism to report grievances related to the App.

1. Our Commitment

DocuScan OCR is committed to addressing any concerns, complaints, or grievances from its users in a timely, fair, and transparent manner. This Grievance Redressal Policy outlines the process for submitting and resolving grievances related to the App, its operation, or any content processed through it.

2. Scope of This Policy

This Grievance Redressal Policy covers complaints and concerns regarding:

  • Violation of your privacy rights by the App or its developer.
  • Unauthorized access to or disclosure of your data through the App.
  • Technical issues that result in data loss, corruption, or inaccessibility.
  • Violation of any applicable laws by the App, including the Information Technology Act, 2000 (India) and rules thereunder.
  • Objectionable, inappropriate, or infringing content that may be processed or stored through the App.
  • Any other concern related to the App's operation or your use of it.

3. How to File a Grievance

If you have a grievance, please submit it in writing to the Grievance Officer with the following information:

  • Your name and contact information (email address, phone number).
  • Your relationship to the App (e.g., end user, potential user, affected third party).
  • A detailed description of the grievance, including the date and time of the incident (if applicable).
  • Any supporting documents or evidence that substantiate your grievance.
  • The specific relief or resolution you are seeking.

5. Grievance Resolution Process

  1. Acknowledgement: Upon receiving a grievance, the Grievance Officer will send an acknowledgement within 24 hours, confirming receipt and providing a reference number for tracking.
  2. Initial Review: The Grievance Officer will conduct an initial review of the grievance to determine its nature and validity. This review will be completed within 3 business days.
  3. Investigation: Based on the initial review, if a detailed investigation is warranted, the Grievance Officer will investigate the matter. This may include reviewing App logs (if available), checking code, or contacting the user for additional information.
  4. Resolution: The Grievance Officer will communicate the resolution or proposed action within 15 days of receipt. The resolution may include, depending on the nature of the grievance:
    • A technical fix for bugs or data issues.
    • Removal of or action on reported content (if applicable).
    • An explanation of why the reported issue does not constitute a violation or error.
    • Escalation to applicable law enforcement or regulatory authorities (if required by law).
  5. Escalation: If you are not satisfied with the resolution, you may escalate the matter to the appropriate judicial or regulatory authority, including the relevant adjudicating officer under the Information Technology Act, 2000.

7. Reporting Illegal or Objectionable Content

As an offline-first document scanner, DocuScan OCR does not host, store, or transmit user content on its own servers. User documents are stored locally on the device and are uploaded to third-party cloud services (Google Drive, Dropbox, Cloudflare R2) only at the user's explicit direction.

If you believe that a user's documents, processed through the App and uploaded to cloud storage, contain illegal or objectionable content (e.g., child sexual abuse material, terrorist content, or content violating applicable laws), please:

  1. Report the content directly to the relevant cloud service provider (Google, Dropbox, or Cloudflare) through their own reporting mechanisms. Those providers have their own grievance and content moderation processes.
  2. Copy the Grievance Officer on your report for awareness and coordination, but note that the developer does not have access to user cloud storage and cannot directly remove content from those services.

8. False or Frivolous Complaints

Users who file false or frivolous grievances may be reported to the appropriate authorities. The Grievance Officer reserves the right to reject grievances that are found to be malicious, abusive, or without reasonable basis after an initial review.
