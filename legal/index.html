TL;DR: DocuScan OCR is a free, offline-first document scanner that does not collect, store, or process your data on any backend server. No analytics, no telemetry, no advertising ID, no account creation required. The pages below cover privacy, terms of use, security, accessibility, and legal compliance for all users globally.

Legal Pages

Privacy Policy Required

What data the app collects (nothing except what you explicitly choose to sync), how OAuth tokens are handled, third-party services (Google Drive, Dropbox, Cloudflare R2), data retention, and your rights under GDPR, CCPA, and India's DPDP Act.

Terms of Service Required

License terms, acceptable use, disclaimers, limitation of liability, governing law (India), dispute resolution (arbitration provision), and terms for cloud services.

Security Policy & Responsible Disclosure Recommended

Full encryption architecture (Android Keystore + PBKDF2 + AES-256-GCM), threat model covering 9 attack scenarios with residual risk ratings, known security limitations, and guidelines for responsible vulnerability disclosure.

Acceptable Use Policy Recommended

Prohibited uses of the App, including illegal content, intellectual property violations, harmful activities, and enforcement provisions.

Grievance Redressal Policy India IT Act

Grievance Officer contact, filing process, resolution timelines (24h acknowledge, 15 day resolve), and escalation path, compliant with Indian IT Act and IT Rules 2021.

Cookie Policy Informational

Disclaimer Recommended

No warranty, no liability for OCR accuracy, no medical/legal/financial advice via the app, export disclaimer, and forward-looking statements.

Refund Policy Informational

The app is free with no paid features. This page covers why no refunds are applicable and disclaimers for donations, if any.

Cancellation Policy Informational

Covering cancellation of cloud sync connections, account unlinking, and the non-applicability of subscription cancellation (no subscriptions exist).

Accessibility Statement Recommended

Commitment to accessibility, WCAG conformance status, known limitations (camera viewfinder, swipe gestures, OCR text), testing approach, and how to report accessibility barriers.
